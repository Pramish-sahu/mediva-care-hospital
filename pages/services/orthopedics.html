Department overview

Restore movement, reduce pain, and return to daily life

Our Orthopedics Department evaluates injuries and conditions affecting bones, joints, muscles, tendons, ligaments, and the spine. The goal is to identify the cause of pain or reduced mobility and choose the least invasive effective care pathway.

Treatment may involve education, activity modification, medicines, injections, physiotherapy, bracing, minimally invasive procedures, or surgery. Rehabilitation is planned from the start so recovery goals remain practical and measurable.

  • Function-led evaluationAssessment considers pain, strength, stability, movement, work, sport, and daily activities.
  • Conservative-first optionsNon-surgical care is considered where appropriate before procedural or surgical treatment.
  • Planned rehabilitationTherapy and recovery milestones are coordinated with the orthopedic treatment plan.

Care designed around mobility goals

Whether the goal is walking comfortably, returning to work, recovering from injury, or resuming sport, treatment is built around safe function and long-term joint health.

When to seek care

Symptoms worth discussing with a specialist

An orthopedic consultation can help when pain, injury, stiffness, weakness, or reduced movement is not improving or is interfering with daily life.

Joint pain or stiffness

Persistent pain, swelling, locking, or reduced movement in a joint.

Sports injury

Sprains, ligament injuries, muscle or tendon pain, or repeated activity-related symptoms.

Possible fracture

Pain, swelling, deformity, or inability to use a limb after injury.

Back or neck pain

Persistent spinal pain, pain radiating to a limb, or mobility limitations.

Hand or shoulder problems

Weakness, restricted motion, tingling, or pain affecting work and daily tasks.

Difficulty walking

Limping, instability, repeated falls, or reduced walking tolerance.

Some injuries need urgent assessment

Seek urgent care for an open injury, obvious deformity, severe uncontrolled pain, loss of limb sensation or movement, heavy bleeding, or a suspected serious spine injury. Call Emergency Desk.
