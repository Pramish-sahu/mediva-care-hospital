Department overview

Healthcare that grows with your child

The Pediatrics Department supports children from birth through adolescence with preventive care, acute illness assessment, chronic-condition follow-up, nutrition guidance, developmental review, and family education.

Our child-friendly approach combines careful examination with age-appropriate communication. Parents and caregivers are included in decisions, and treatment instructions are explained clearly for safe care at home.

  • Age-appropriate assessmentClinical evaluation is adapted for newborns, infants, children, and adolescents.
  • Preventive health focusWell-child visits support growth, development, nutrition, vaccination, and safety.
  • Family-centered communicationCaregivers receive clear explanations, warning signs, and practical follow-up guidance.

Friendly spaces, trained pediatric teams, and simple explanations help reduce anxiety while allowing careful and respectful assessment.

When to seek care

Symptoms worth discussing with a specialist

Contact a pediatrician when a child is unwell, symptoms are worsening, feeding or hydration is affected, or a parent is concerned about development or behavior.

Fever or infection symptoms

Fever, persistent cough, ear pain, sore throat, or recurring infections.

Breathing concerns

Wheezing, fast breathing, persistent cough, or increased effort to breathe.

Feeding or digestion problems

Poor feeding, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, abdominal pain, or dehydration concerns.

Growth concerns

Questions about weight, height, nutrition, appetite, puberty, or development.

Skin or allergy symptoms

Rashes, eczema, hives, recurrent allergy symptoms, or suspected triggers.

Behavior or development

Concerns about speech, learning, sleep, attention, movement, or social development.

Urgent symptoms in a child need prompt help

Severe breathing difficulty, blue or grey lips, poor responsiveness, a seizure, serious injury, heavy bleeding, or a very unwell young infant needs immediate emergency assessment. Call Emergency Desk.

Frequently asked questions

Helpful information before your visit

These general answers support preparation but do not replace an individual medical assessment.

Routine visit timing depends on age and individual needs. Newborns and infants are seen more often, while older children generally need regular preventive reviews. Your pediatrician will advise a suitable schedule.

Bring vaccination and growth records, previous prescriptions or reports, a list of medicines or supplements, and notes about symptoms, feeding, sleep, urine output, or behavior.

A mild illness may not always require postponement, but the clinical team should assess the child and review the specific vaccine. Contact the pediatric clinic for individualized advice.
