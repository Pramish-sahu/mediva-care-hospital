Department overview

Complete care for heart health at every stage

The Cardiology Department supports adults with heart symptoms, known cardiovascular conditions, and preventive-care needs. Our clinical teams combine detailed consultation with modern testing so patients understand both the diagnosis and the next step.

Care plans are personalized around symptoms, medical history, risk factors, test findings, and lifestyle priorities. When procedures are needed, cardiologists work closely with critical-care, anaesthesia, imaging, rehabilitation, and nursing teams.

  • Prevention-first assessmentRisk review for blood pressure, cholesterol, diabetes, family history, and lifestyle factors.
  • Coordinated diagnosticsTesting is selected according to clinical need and reviewed by the treating cardiologist.
  • Clear treatment planningPatients and families receive practical explanations, options, and follow-up guidance.

From initial assessment and diagnostic testing to intervention, monitored recovery, and rehabilitation, the care team remains connected across the patient journey.

When to seek care

Symptoms worth discussing with a specialist

Book a cardiology consultation for persistent or recurring symptoms, especially when they are new, worsening, or linked with exertion.

Chest discomfort

Pressure, tightness, heaviness, or pain that needs medical assessment.

Shortness of breath

Breathlessness at rest, during activity, or when lying down.

Palpitations

A racing, fluttering, skipping, or unusually slow heartbeat.

Swelling

Persistent swelling in the feet, ankles, legs, or abdomen.

Dizziness or fainting

Unexplained light-headedness, near-fainting, or loss of consciousness.

High blood pressure

Repeated elevated readings or difficulty controlling known hypertension.

Do not wait for an appointment during a possible heart emergency

Severe or persistent chest pain, major breathing difficulty, collapse, or sudden cold sweating can require immediate emergency assessment. Call the local emergency number or Mediva emergency desk now. Call Emergency Desk.

Specialist care built around clinical need

Services are selected after clinical assessment and may include preventive, diagnostic, medical, interventional, and rehabilitation care.

Preventive cardiology

Individual risk assessment, blood-pressure and lipid review, lifestyle guidance, and ongoing prevention planning.

Non-invasive diagnostics

ECG, echocardiography, ambulatory rhythm monitoring, and exercise-based testing when clinically appropriate.

Detailed assessment
03

Coronary evaluation

Specialist review for suspected coronary artery disease, including imaging or angiography when indicated.

Evidence-led planning
04

Interventional cardiology

Catheter-based procedures such as angioplasty and stenting for selected patients after informed evaluation.

Minimally invasive care
05

Heart rhythm care

Assessment of palpitations and arrhythmias, medication review, monitoring, and device planning when required.

Rhythm management
06

Heart failure & rehabilitation

Multidisciplinary symptom management, education, monitored recovery, and long-term follow-up.
