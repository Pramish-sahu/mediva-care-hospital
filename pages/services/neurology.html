Department overview

Thoughtful assessment for complex neurological symptoms

Neurological symptoms can affect movement, sensation, speech, memory, balance, sleep, and daily independence. Our neurologists take time to understand the pattern, timing, triggers, medical history, and impact of each concern.

Assessment may include a detailed neurological examination, selected imaging, laboratory tests, or neurophysiology. Care plans focus on diagnosis, symptom control, risk reduction, rehabilitation, and clear follow-up.

  • Detailed clinical evaluationHistory and examination are used to identify the most appropriate diagnostic direction.
  • Integrated testingImaging and neurophysiology are coordinated only when they add useful clinical information.
  • Rehabilitation partnershipPhysiotherapy, speech, occupational, and cognitive support can be included when required.

Care beyond a single consultation

Neurological conditions often require observation over time. Our teams coordinate medication review, symptom tracking, rehabilitation, family education, and planned reassessment.

When to seek care

Symptoms worth discussing with a specialist

Seek neurological assessment when symptoms are persistent, unexplained, recurrent, progressive, or affecting safety and daily function.

Frequent or severe headaches

New headache patterns, migraine, or headaches with unusual neurological features.

Seizures or blackouts

Episodes of shaking, staring, altered awareness, or unexplained loss of consciousness.

Numbness or weakness

Persistent tingling, altered sensation, limb weakness, or loss of coordination.

Balance or movement changes

Tremor, stiffness, slowed movement, falls, or difficulty walking.

Speech or memory concerns

Changes in speech, understanding, memory, behavior, or concentration.

Vision or facial symptoms

Double vision, facial weakness, drooping, or unexplained visual disturbance.

Sudden neurological symptoms are an emergency

Sudden facial droop, arm weakness, speech difficulty, severe new headache, seizure with poor recovery, or sudden loss of balance or vision needs immediate emergency care. Call Emergency Desk.
