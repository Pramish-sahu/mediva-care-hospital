Department overview

Protect natural teeth and restore confident smiles

The Dental Care Department provides preventive, restorative, cosmetic, and surgical oral healthcare for adults and children. Every visit begins with a careful examination and a clear explanation of findings and treatment choices.

Our approach prioritizes prevention and preservation of natural teeth wherever possible. When repair or replacement is needed, treatment is planned around oral health, comfort, function, appearance, and long-term maintenance.

  • Prevention and early detectionRoutine examinations, hygiene guidance, and timely treatment help reduce future oral-health problems.
  • Transparent treatment plansPatients receive clear options, expected steps, aftercare, and cost guidance before treatment.
  • Comfort-conscious dentistryThe team uses gentle techniques, appropriate pain control, and supportive communication.

Whole-mouth treatment planning

Teeth, gums, bite, jaw function, appearance, and general health are considered together so treatment remains practical and sustainable.

When to seek care

Symptoms worth discussing with a specialist

Arrange a dental evaluation for pain, swelling, bleeding, sensitivity, damage, or changes in the mouth that do not resolve.

Tooth pain or sensitivity

Pain with biting, temperature, sweets, or spontaneous throbbing discomfort.

Bleeding or swollen gums

Gum bleeding, tenderness, recession, persistent bad breath, or loose teeth.

Facial or jaw swelling

Swelling near a tooth, jaw, cheek, or under the chin needs timely assessment.

Broken or injured tooth

Cracks, chips, knocked-out teeth, or dental trauma after an accident.

Missing or loose teeth

Difficulty chewing, changes in bite, or concerns about tooth replacement.

Mouth sores or jaw problems

Ulcers that persist, jaw clicking, limited opening, or unexplained oral changes.

Dental swelling can become urgent

Rapidly increasing facial swelling, difficulty breathing or swallowing, uncontrolled bleeding, serious facial trauma, or a knocked-out permanent tooth needs urgent professional care. Call Emergency Desk.
